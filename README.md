## How I Think About AI Engineering

I care about more than getting a model to answer once in a demo.

A useful AI system needs the right data, clear boundaries, thoughtful evaluation, secure access, predictable failure handling, and a path to production. That means understanding the workflow first, then choosing the right mix of retrieval, agents, APIs, orchestration, observability, and infrastructure.

The questions I usually start with are:

- What business decision or workflow should this improve?
- What trusted data should ground the response?
- What can the AI safely do on its own, and what needs approval?
- How will retrieval, generation, agent behavior, and business outcomes be evaluated?
- How will the system handle latency, retries, cost, scale, and failure?
- How will authentication, authorization, tenant isolation, and tool access be enforced?
